Seoul

10.025.800 Resident

South Korea

37° 25' - 37° 41' LU, 126° 45'- 127° 11' BT

Population Characteristics:

The population of Seoul City exhibits social characteristics asa densely populated, ethnically homogeneous, urban society heavily influenced by Confucian values ​​emphasizing hard work, respect, and social hierarchy.

Nearly half of South Korea's total population lives in the Seoul metropolitan area, making it one of the most densely populated cities in the world. This high population density results in a fast-paced and competitive city life.

The majority of Seoul's population is ethnic Korean. Although there are small minorities of expatriates, Japanese, and Chinese, the city as a whole has a very homogeneous population.

City's Unique Identity:

Cultural icons embody the non-physical, dynamic essence of Seoul's identity. The most globally significant is the Korean Wave (Hallyu), encompassing K-Pop and K-dramas, which has turned Seoul into a worldwide entertainment hub and trendsetter in fashion and lifestyle. Hanbok, the traditional Korean clothing, remains a vital cultural symbol, connecting the city's modern life with its historical roots. Hansik, or traditional Korean food including kimchi, is another core icon reflecting national identity and customs. Furthermore, the creation of the unique alphabet, Hangul, is a significant source of national pride. Distinct urban cultural aspects also include the widespread " palli-palli " (hurry-hurry) ethos of efficiency and the vibrant PC-bang (internet cafe) gaming culture.

Landmarks are the recognizable physical features and historical or architectural sites that define the city's skyline and geography. The primary historical landmarks are the Grand Palaces, particularly Gyeongbokgung Palace , the largest royal palace from the Joseon Dynasty, and the UNESCO-listed Changdeokgung Palace , which showcases Korea's rich royal history.

Towering over the city, the Namsan Tower (N Seoul Tower) is an iconic modern landmark offering panoramic views and a popular destination for visitors. In contrast to the ancient palaces, the futuristic Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP) , an architectural marvel, represents the city's focus on innovative design and is a hub for fashion and art. The tranquil Cheonggyecheon Stream , a successful urban restoration project, serves as a prominent natural landmark within the bustling city center. For a blend of history and architecture, the preserved traditional houses (hanok) in Bukchon Hanok Village offer a unique glimpse into the past amidst the surrounding modern high-rises. The super-tall Lotte World Tower is the definitive modern skyscraper landmark, dominating the contemporary cityscape.

  • Gangnam Festival : An annual celebration in the Gangnam district featuring musical performances, traditional dances, and contemporary art.
  • Seoul Lantern Festival : An annual event featuring traditional and modern lanterns along the Cheonggyecheon Stream, often coinciding with the Gwanghwamun Christmas Market.
  • Seoul Street Art Festival : A festival featuring various street art performances.
  • Seoul Intangible Cultural Heritage Festival : Showcasing traditional culture at Namsangol Hanok Village.
  • Seoul Festa : A cultural festival featuring K-Pop music, fashion, food, and other exciting events throughout the city.

The culinary scene in Seoul is very diverse, ranging from traditional dishes such as bulgogi (grilled beef), bibimbap (mixed rice), and samgye-tang (ginseng chicken), to popular street foods such as tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), kimbap (rice rolls), and odeng (fish satay) Visitors can also find chimaek (fried chicken and beer), seolleongtang (beef bone soup), and various seafood dishes at traditional markets.

The main local languages ​​spoken in Seoul are Korean language(Hanguk-uh), which is also the official language of South Korea.

Specifically, Seoul residents speak the Seoul dialect , also known as the Gyeonggi dialect . This dialect is the prestige (standard) dialect in South Korea and serves as the basis for the standard form of Korean used in formal education, mass media (television, radio, film), and official communications throughout the country.

Although there are several other regional dialects in South Korea (such as the Jeju, Gyeongsang, and Jeolla dialects), the Seoul dialect is the most commonly learned and heard, especially by foreigners studying Korean.

The Role of Cities in a National or Regional Context:

  • Political and Administrative Center: As the capital, Seoul is the center of government, where most of the executive, legislative, and judicial branches are located.
  • Economic and Business Center: The Seoul metropolitan area (including Incheon and Gyeonggi) is the country's economic engine. It is the headquarters of many of South Korea's chaebols (large conglomerates), and a center of finance, commerce, and industry.
  • Cultural and Historical Center: The city is rich in historical heritage, including five ancient palaces and UNESCO World Heritage Sites, while also being a vibrant center of modern culture. Seoul is the birthplace and epicenter of the global spread of "Hallyu," or the Korean Wave (K-pop, Korean dramas, etc.).
  • Technology and Innovation Hub: Seoul is known as one of the most digitally connected cities in the world, with modern infrastructure and advanced technology industries centered in areas such as Gangnam and Digital Media City.
  • Education and Transportation Hub: The city has leading educational institutions and a highly efficient public transportation system (rail, subway, airport), connecting all corners of the country.

City Development Focus:

Seoul has earned global recognition as one of the best entertainment destinations. The Seoul City Government announced on November 13, 2025, that Seoul was ranked second in the world for the best entertainment destination.

The award was presented at the Tourise Awards held on November 11, 2025 (local time) in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. The Tourise Awards is an international event held for the first time in 2025 to encourage innovation and growth in the global tourism industry. The awards are organized by the Saudi Arabian Ministry of Tourism and supported by the Saudi Arabian central government.

There are five award categories: entertainment, arts/culture, food/culinary, shopping, and adventure. Winners are selected through an assessment by a panel of judges consisting of top experts and analysis of global tourism data.

Seoul placed second behind Tokyo (Japan) in the entertainment category. In addition, Seoul also entered the top 20 in the food/culinary and shopping categories.

The organizers explained that Seoul's strengths are its blend of innovation and culture, the global influence of K-pop, its entertainment industry with cutting-edge technology, and its nightlife that leads global trends.

The cities that achieved first place in each category were New York (United States) for arts/culture, Tokyo for food/culinary, Paris (France) for shopping, and Ancash (Peru) for adventure.

Seoul has won various awards in 2025 and has successfully established itself as a global tourism city. Some of the awards won include the Most Loved City by Millennials and Generation Z by Trazy Travel for four consecutive years, the Best City for Solo Travel by Trip Advisor, and Asia's Best Leisure Destination by Global Traveler.

  • TOPIS (Seoul Transport Operation and Information Service): This is a centralized traffic management system that manages urban mobility in real time. It helps optimize bus routes, manages traffic signals, and provides accurate transportation information to citizens, thereby reducing congestion.
  • The "Sharing City Seoul" project: Launched in 2012, this program aims to develop a sharing economy encompassing infrastructure, public resources, and digital services. The goal is to address challenges such as overcrowding, pollution, and overconsumption by facilitating the sharing of resources such as coworking spaces, transportation, and other goods, thereby enhancing sustainability.
  • Fast and Affordable Internet Infrastructure: Seoul is renowned for having one of the fastest and most affordable internet access in the world, which is widely available even in public places like subways and parks.
  • Fast and Affordable Internet Infrastructure: Seoul is renowned for having one of the fastest and most affordable internet access in the world, which is widely available even in public places like subways and parks.
  • Community-Centered Urban Regeneration Project: Instead of simply undertaking extensive new development, Seoul is implementing an urban regeneration policy designed to encourage citizen participation and harmonious coexistence, focusing on sustainable and community-oriented renewal.

  • The Seoul Metropolitan Government has launched a “Garden City Seoul” initiative, converting old, “gray” downtown structures into ecological public parks, gardens, and green corridors. As of 2022, Seoul’s total park area has grown significantly, improving per capita access to greenery. By 2026, the city plans to build a 2,063-km “Seoul Green Path” linking green spaces, trails, and streams to connect people with nature more easily.
  • Seoul announced a five-year climate plan that includes restoring 75 urban streams, increasing riverside forests, and creating “smart water circulation” zones to reuse rainwater and groundwater. This “Stream Renaissance” effort helps cool the city, improves water resilience, and reduces the urban heat island effect.
  • Seoul is promoting zero-emission transport, particularly electric and hydrogen vehicles. Subsidies for EVs, expansion of EV charging infrastructure, and use of big data / smart governance help reduce air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions. The city also aims to expand cycling and walking paths, reduce road space for cars, and make public transport more accessible.
