Jakarta

11.038.216 Resident

Indonesia

106°49' BT dan 6°12' LS

Population Characteristics:

The social characteristics of Jakarta's population are defined by intense heterogeneity and pluralism, stemming from its status as a major economic and political hub that attracts diverse ethnicities from across Indonesia and the world.

This environment fosters a predominantly individualistic, rational, and somewhat impersonal gesellschaft society, where relationships are often transactional and focused on professional or economic interests. Despite a high level of tolerance for diversity and openness to modernization and global trends, the city grapples with the challenges of significant social inequality and the fast-paced, competitive urban lifestyle driven by high population density and constant migration.

City's Unique Identity:

The cultural icons of the city of Jakarta which were officially designated through the Regulation of the Governor of DKI Jakarta No. 11 of 2017 include:ondel-ondel , coconut flowers , tooth ornaments , sadariah clothes , kebaya kerancang , Betawi batik , kerak telor , and bir pletok . In addition, the National Monument (Monas) and the Jakarta Fair (PRJ) are also often considered cultural icons of Jakarta.

  • National Monument (Monas): Jakarta's premier icon, a 132-meter-tall monument symbolizing Indonesia's struggle for independence. Its summit is adorned with a golden flame.
  • Istiqlal Mosque: The largest mosque in Southeast Asia and one of Indonesia's most important religious centers. Located across from the National Monument (Monas).
  • Bundaran HI and Welcome Monument: Located at the intersection of Jalan MH Thamrin, this landmark is often a gathering point for celebrations and a symbol of Jakarta's annual festivities.
  • Jakarta Old Town : A historic area with buildings from the Dutch era such as the Fatahillah Museum and Sunda Kelapa Harbor.
  • Gelora Bung Karno Stadium : One of the largest sports stadiums in Indonesia, often used for sporting events and large concerts.
  • Dirgantara Statue (Pancoran Statue): A monument known for its statues of a man and a woman looking up to the sky, symbolizing human courage and strength.

Some of the big festivals in Jakarta areJakarta Fair Kemayoran (exhibition, culinary, music), Jakarta Fashion Week (fashion festival), Jakarta Great Sale Festival (shopping promotion), and Jakarta Investment Festival (investment festival). In addition, there are also other events such as the Joyland Festival (music), We The Fest (music), Jakarta Marathon (marathon), and the Jakarta Light Festival (light art).

Jakarta’s culinary scene is defined by rich flavors and iconic dishes such as Nasi Uduk, fragrant coconut-milk rice served with fried chicken or balado eggs, and Soto Betawi, a signature creamy beef soup. Popular street foods like Gado-gado and Ketoprak showcase fresh vegetables, tofu, and rice cakes paired with Jakarta’s distinctive peanut-based sauces. Comfort staples such as Chicken Porridge offer savory warmth with toppings like shredded chicken and crackers, while traditional specialties like Kerak Telor highlight Betawi heritage through its crisp blend of sticky rice, eggs, and coconut. Completing the experience, Martabak Pecenongan is widely celebrated for its indulgent sweet and savory varieties, solidifying Jakarta’s reputation as a vibrant culinary destination.

The local language of Jakarta City isBetawi language, a dialect of Malay and a hallmark of Betawi culture. This language has been passed down through generations and has distinctive characteristics, such as the use of the words "gue" and "elo." Besides Betawi, other languages ​​are also spoken in Jakarta due to its ethnic diversity, such as Sundanese, Bugis, and Mandarin.

The Role of Cities in a National or Regional Context:

Jakarta plays a central role in the national and regional context as the core economic, growth, and employment hub of the Jabodetabek metropolitan area.

Its strong economic pull is reflected in significant daily commuting flows, both within Jakarta’s administrative regions and between Jakarta and surrounding cities. According to the 2023 Jabodetabek Commuter Statistics by BPS, internal commuter movement is especially high in East Jakarta, with 1.19 million commuters, while inter-city flows—particularly between East Jakarta–South Jakarta, East Jakarta–Central Jakarta, West Jakarta–Central Jakarta, and South Jakarta–Central Jakarta—indicate the concentration of residential areas and major activity centers. Regionally, 1.5 million commuters travel from Bogor, Depok, Tangerang, and Bekasi (Bodetabek) to Jakarta, highlighting the city’s dominant role as a regional employment center but also creating pressure on urban infrastructure such as clean water, wastewater, waste management, electricity, transportation, and energy services.

These commuting patterns influence settlement trends in surrounding regions, driving the emergence of new activity centers as well as environmental challenges. At the same time, Jakarta’s strong economic performance—reflected through its Gross Regional Domestic Product (GRDP) growth—positions the city as a key driver of Indonesia’s economic welfare. Economic indicators show not only growth but also efforts toward equitable distribution, underscoring Jakarta’s importance in shaping economic well-being and regional development across the country.

City Development Focus:

Development of tourism in Jakartafocus on utilizing the tourism potential of metropolitan cities, such as culinary, history (National Museum, Old Town), Betawi culture, and nature (mangrove areas)The government is striving to improve tourism to be more high-quality, inclusive, and sustainable through strategies such as business promotion and MICE (Meetings, Incentives, Conventions, and Exhibitions) tourism, as well as visitor management to maintain environmental balance and comfort. While challenges such as traffic congestion remain, strategic efforts are underway to establish Jakarta as a world-class global destination.

  • The room occupancy rate in star-rated hotels in Jakarta in March 2025 reached 38.26 percent, down 5.82 percentage points (year-on-year).
  • The room occupancy rate in non-starred hotels in Jakarta in March 2025 reached 36.31 percent, down 1.33 percentage points (year-on-year).
  • The number of domestic tourist trips to DKI Jakarta reached 23,175.04 thousand trips in the first quarter of 2025, an increase of 17.74 percent compared to the same period the previous year.

The main innovations and superior policies of the Jakarta Provincial Government (Pemprov) are in the field of integrated transportation and digital transformation through the Smart City concept (Jakarta Smart City).

  • 1. Transportation Sector (Smart Mobility)
    • Integrated Transportation Modes (JakLingko): Jakarta is developing an integrated mass transportation system, including the MRT, LRT, TransJakarta, and KRL, under the JakLingko umbrella. This innovation includes a single payment system for various transportation modes, facilitating citizen mobility.
    • Mass Transportation Infrastructure Development: The construction and development of the MRT and LRT networks are the backbone of transportation in Jakarta to reduce dependence on private vehicles.
  • 2. Digital and Public Service Sector (Smart Governance & Smart Living)
    • Jakarta Smart City (JSC): JSC is a platform that provides technology-based solutions to support digital transformation in the public sector, including citizen services through various applications. This increases the transparency and efficiency of public services.
    • Integrated Service Applications: The DKI Jakarta Provincial Government has launched various applications to facilitate access to services, such as online licensing , disaster information, and public complaints, all of which are connected within the city's digital ecosystem.
  • 3. Environmental Sector (Smart Environment)
    • Jakarta Recycle Center (JRC): Innovation in waste management by prioritizing waste sorting at the source and involving active community participation through waste banks and donation platforms.
    • Waste Management Technology Development: Construction of the largest waste management facility in Indonesia using high technology to reduce the waste burden in the Capital City.
    • Jakarta Low Emission Program: A policy to facilitate clean water needs and control flooding, as well as encourage a more environmentally friendly urban lifestyle.

  • Green Infrastructure & Sustainable Transport: According to a press release from EIB Global, Jakarta is developing greener, more sustainable commuting options through transport network expansion. This supports over 10 million residents, reduces travel times, and helps cut air and noise pollution.
  • Disaster / Climate Capacity Building: The DKI Jakarta Environmental Agency is organizing capacity-building programs on climate resilience with other Southeast Asian cities, to deal with shared climate risks (sea level rise, floods, etc.).
  • City Diplomacy & Global Climate Finance: Jakarta has joined major global climate networks (such as C40) and committed to phasing out fossil-fuel investments in city assets in favor of green investments.

Opportunities for Collaboration:

  • Jakarta has joined the C40 Clean Investment Accelerator, which supports cities globally (including in Europe, Asia, Latin America) to attract climate finance and build resilient, climate-friendly infrastructure.

  • Research shows Jakarta’s city government actively collaborates with other cities through C40 to tackle cross-border climate issues like urbanization, mobility, and environmental changes.

  • Through the “Urban-Act” program (funded by Germany’s IKI), Jakarta is working with other Southeast Asian cities (e.g., through UCLG-ASPAC) to promote low-carbon, climate-resilient urban development.

  • Jakarta hosted the 2024 International Mayors Forum with delegates from 33 countries and 63 city governments from Asia, Africa, Latin America, and more. The forum focused on sustainable development and SDGs.

Jakarta, officially the Special Capital Region of Jakarta, is the capital and largest city of Indonesia and an autonomous region at the provincial level. Lying on the northwest coast of Java, the world's most populous island, Jakarta is the largest metropole in Southeast Asia and serves as the Headquarters of ASEAN.
