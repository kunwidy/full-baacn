Tehran

8.700.000 Resident

Iran

41’35° LU 26’51° BT

Population Characteristics:

The population of Tehran, the capital of Iran, has unique social characteristics as the largest metropolitan center in the country, which is dominated by Persian culture and Shia Islam.

The vast majority of Tehran's population is Shia Muslim, which is also the official religion of the Iranian state (approximately 90-95% of the total Iranian population). Religious life is deeply tied to political and social identity in Iran. There are also recognized religious minorities, such as Zoroastrians, Christians, and Jews, who have the right to practice their religion within certain limits set by the government. Tehran has grown from a small town into a large and dense metropolis, with a metropolitan population of over 9 million. This demonstrates the existence of a modern urban society with all its complexities.

City's Unique Identity:

Tehran's cultural icons encompass aspects of Persian history, art, and traditions deeply rooted in the Iranian capital. They reflect the rich heritage of Persian civilization and the modern Islamic Republic of Iran.

  • Persian Rug Art (Carpet): The intricate and world-renowned craft of carpet weaving is an important part of Iranian culture and art, which can often be found in Tehran's Grand Bazaar or the Iran Carpet Museum.
  • Golestan Palace :Although a physical structure,Golestan Palaceis a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is a symbol of the legacy of the Qajar dynasty, showcasing a distinctive blend of Persian architecture and European influences, making it a cultural icon rich in history.
  • Persian Poetry and Literature: The high regard for classical poets such as Rumi and Hafez is a profound aspect of the culture, where literature remains an important part of Iranian national identity.
  • Local Cuisine and Cafe/Bazaar Lifestyle: A strong social culture revolves around traditional tea houses (chaikhaneh), modern cafes, and bustling markets like the Grand Bazaar or Tajrish Bazaar , where social interaction and local cuisine thrive.
  • Iranian Celebrations and Festivals: Annual festivals such as Nowruz (Persian New Year) and Yalda Night are an integral part of Tehran's cultural life, reflecting its ancient Zoroastrian roots.

Tehran's city landmarks are visually or geographically prominent physical structures that serve as markers of location or orientation in the cityscape . They are often prominent because of their architecture or size.

  • Azadi Tower (Freedom Tower) : An iconic monument built in 1971 to commemorate the 2,500th anniversary of the Persian Empire. It isTehran's most recognizable symbol and is often considered the gateway to the city from the west.
  • Milad Tower : A multi-purpose tower that is the sixth-tallest free-standing tower in the world. Its height (435 meters) makes it a skyline feature visible from almost every corner of the city.
  • Tabiat Bridge : A unique and large modern pedestrian bridge connecting two public parks (Taleghani and Abo-Atash)over a major highway. Its innovative architectural design makes it a contemporary landmark.
  • Tehran's Grand Bazaar : Although also a cultural center, its nature as a vast and historic physical market makes it a major physical landmark in the southern part of the city.
  • Sa'dabad and Niavaran Palace Complex : A large palace complex north of Tehran that served as the residence of the last Qajar and Pahlavi dynasties, featuring distinctive architecture and a museum.

Traditional and Cultural Festivals:

  • Nowruz (Persian New Year) : This is the most important festival in Iran, marking the beginning of spring and the turn of the Persian calendar year (around March 21). The celebration lasts for two weeks and involves various rituals, including preparing the "Haft-sin" table and performing arts and cultural performances.
  • Chaharshanbe Suri (Fire Festival) : Celebrated on the last Wednesday night before Nowruz, where people jump over bonfires and set off fireworks to ward off evil.
  • Mehregan (Persian Autumn Festival) : Although not as festive as Nowruz, this ancient festival, also known as “Persian Thanksgiving,” is a celebration of autumn and the harvest.
  • Shab-e Yalda (Yalda Night) : A celebration of the longest night of the year (around December 21st), where families gather to read poetry and eat fruits such as watermelon and pomegranate.

Modern Arts and Events Festival:

  • Tehran Flower and Rose Water Festival : An annual event showcasing various types of flowers and rose water at the National Botanical Garden or the city's cultural centers, usually taking place around May.
  • Tehran Contemporary Sound Festival (TCS) : A festival focusing on contemporary music and sound.
  • Traditional Ritual Performance Festival : A cultural event featuring traditional Iranian drama and rituals.
  • Tehran Short Film Festival : An international film festival that attracts thousands of films from various countries.
  • International Puppet Festival : An event held to celebrate the art of puppetry.

Tehran's cuisine includes a variety of main dishes, such as kebabs (including kubideh and jujeh ), rich stews such as ghormeh sabzi , and rice dishes such as tahchi. Popular desserts include sholeh zard (saffron rice pudding) and traditional drinks such as doogh (a yogurt drink) and chai (Persian tea).

The main local language in Tehran isTehran Persian( Tehran dialect ), which is a variant of Western Persian. This dialect is used in everyday conversation and is the most common dialect in Iran. Besides the Tehran dialect, Tehran Persian is the common form of Persian spoken in the Iranian capital, although it is officially referred to as Farsi.

The Role of Cities in a National or Regional Context:

  • Political and Governmental Center: Tehran is the country's administrative center, housing the presidential office, parliament, supreme court, and key ministries. Important political decisions affecting the entire country are made here.
  • Economic Center: This city dominates Iran's economy. Tehran is a major industrial center, with significant contributions from the oil sector (which accounts for the majority of foreign exchange earnings), manufacturing, and services. The Tehran Stock Exchange is also the center of the country's financial activity.
  • Demographics and Employment Center: Tehran is the largest labor market and home to approximately one-tenth of Iran's population. The city's growth is driven by its status as an administrative and economic center.
  • Cultural and Historical Center: The city is rich in historical sites from various Iranian dynasties, such as the Golestan Palace and the Sa'dabad complex, which reflect its rich Persian cultural heritage. Tehran is also a meeting point for various ethnic and linguistic groups, although Persian culture and language dominate.

City Development Focus:

Iran, a land of diverse landscapes and rich cultural heritage, offers various natural and historical attractions for every type of traveler. From towering mountains and enchanting forests to unique geological sites and sprawling deserts, Iran promises an unforgettable journey. With 27 UNESCO World Heritage sites, it ranks as the 10th country globally with the most cultural heritage sites. In this blog post, we explore tourism information in Iran.

In 2024, Iran welcomed around 4.16 million foreign tourists, showing a 24% increase compared to the same period in the previous year. This growth has brought Iran closer to its highest tourism record, which was set in 2019 when 8.8 million international tourists visited the country. That year was the last before the COVID-19 pandemic changed global travel patterns.

  • Citizen Services Platform "MyTehran" : An integrated digital services platform that provides citizens with easy access to various city services, enhancing transparency and public participation.
  • Traffic Optimization : Utilizing ICT to adjust traffic light timings to be more efficient and provide real-time traffic information to drivers through navigation applications.
  • Smart Waste Management : An efficient waste collection management system, including the use of technology for more optimal waste collection.
  • Tehran Urban Innovation Center (TUIC) : A center dedicated to nurturing startups and knowledge-based companies, fostering local economic growth in the technology sector.

  • Green Space & Ecosystem Services: Studies show that Tehran has a network of urban green spaces whose connectivity can deliver important ecosystem services to residents, helping with heat mitigation, air quality, and environmental justice.
  • Smart Environmental Management: Tehran’s municipality is implementing “smart” environmental monitoring systems. For example, there is a unified digital system to monitor energy use in municipal buildings (including heating systems and solar installations), which helps reduce energy consumption and carbon emissions.
  • Sustainable and Inclusive Transportation: Research into Tehran’s transport system shows efforts to improve access to public transportation for disadvantaged groups (elderly, children, persons with disabilities) while promoting more sustainable urban mobility.

Opportunities for Collaboration:

Cooperation between Teheran and Bandung holds significant promise to deepen social, economic, and cultural ties.

The research on inclusive transport in Tehran could translate into a cooperation program between Tehran, Bandung, and other cities in Asia and Africa to improve public transportation accessibility, especially for marginalized communities, and to design more equitable, low-carbon mobility strategies.

Tehran’s cultural institutions (like the Peace Museum) and environmental-heritage integration could form the basis of a cultural diplomacy tie with Bandung and KAA cities. They could co-organize forums or exchanges on peace, heritage, and sustainable development, especially drawing on shared history or development goals.

Given the shared sustainability goals, Tehran and Bandung (and other KAA cities) could jointly apply for international climate and development funding (such as from the Green Climate Fund, bilateral donors, or multilateral agencies) to finance urban green infrastructure, nature restoration, and inclusive mobility projects.
