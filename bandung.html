Bandung

2.591.763 Resident

Indonesia

107°36' BT dan 6°55' LS

Population Characteristics:

Bandung comes from the word bendung or dam because the Citarum River was dammed by the lava of Mount Tangkuban Parahu which then formed a lake. The legend told by the old people in Bandung says that the name Bandung was taken from a water vehicle consisting of two boats tied side by side called a bandung boat which was used by the Regent of Bandung, RA Wiranatakusumah II , to sail the Citarum in search of a new district seat to replace the old capital in Dayeuhkolot.

The population of Bandung is characterized by its youthful demographic profile, cultural diversity, and strong educational orientation. As one of Indonesia’s major urban centers, the city attracts students, professionals, and migrants from various regions, creating a socially dynamic environment. Bandung’s population tends to be relatively young, with a high proportion of residents in the productive age group, contributing to its vibrant creative and entrepreneurial sectors.

The presence of numerous universities and research institutions also shapes the city’s human capital, making education and innovation key components of Bandung’s identity.

Additionally, the population reflects a blend of Sundanese cultural heritage and modern urban lifestyles, resulting in a community that values tradition while actively engaging with contemporary trends.

City's Unique Identity:

Bandung's culture is deeply rooted in the traditions of the Sundanese people, the indigenous ethnic group of West Java. One of the most prominent cultural heritages is the Angklung, a traditional musical instrument made of bamboo. Saung Angklung Udjo is an iconic venue where Angklung performances are held regularly, showcasing the beautiful harmony of this instrument. Beyond traditional arts, Bandung is recognized as a "Creative City" by the Ministry of Tourism and Creative Economy, with a vibrant ecosystem supporting various creative economy subsectors, fostered by government initiatives like the Patrakomala digital platform managed by the Bandung City Culture and Tourism Office to support local creators. Other recognized intangible cultural works from the city include Benjang, Reak dogdog, and Sunda Merak Dance.

Bandung’s cultural identity is embodied by a rich tapestry of historic and modern icons. Gedung Sate, with its distinctive “satay skewer” rooftop ornament and neoclassical-Sundanese architecture, stands as the city’s most legendary symbol.

The Bandung Sea of Fire Monument commemorates the heroic act during the 1946 revolution when residents burned parts of the city to prevent its capture, a powerful reminder of Bandung’s resilient spirit.

The Pasupati Bridge, a stunning cable-stayed overpass that spans the Cikapundung Valley, represents Bandung’s modern progress, especially when brightly illuminated at night.

Meanwhile, Gedung Merdeka, the historic Art Deco building on Asia–Africa Street, holds deep diplomatic significance as the venue of the 1955 Asia–Africa Conference, and now serves as a museum.

The Al Jabbar Grand Mosque, with its contemporary design and reflective pool, offers both spiritual calm and architectural grandeur.

Braga Street captures the romance of colonial-era Bandung, lined with vintage cafés, art galleries, and boutiques – making it a living testament to the city’s European-influenced past.

On a hilltop overlooking the city sits Villa Isola, an elegant Art Deco mansion that once belonged to a Dutch media tycoon and now houses a university’s rectorate.

Finally, Persib Bandung and its home stadium, Gelora Bandung Lautan Api (GBLA), symbolize local pride and communal spirit, uniting people through a shared passion for football.

The Bandung City Culture and Tourism Office has a vision to establish Bandung as a city of arts and culture and an international tourist destination, which includes organizing various events. Although specific annual festival information in the provided sources is limited, the city's dynamic creative ecosystem ensures a variety of cultural, art, and fashion events throughout the year, often supported through the Patrakomala platform. The city also engages in international collaborations, such as the local government's contribution to arts and crafts festivals, demonstrating its commitment to global cultural exchange.

Bandung is an internationally renowned culinary paradise, offering a harmonious blend of traditional Sundanese flavors and global influences (Asian, Arabic, European). From high-end restaurants to popular street food, the options are incredibly diverse. Authentic local dishes that are a must-try include Siomay and Batagor (fried fish dumplings and tofu), as well as characteristic snacks like Peuyeum Bandung (fermented cassava). The local cuisine is often referred to as a "viral culinary center" thanks to its innovation and creativity.

The Role of Cities in a National or Regional Context:

Bandung plays a highly strategic role at the provincial, regional, and national levels. As the capital of West Java, it functions as the administrative and political center where major provincial decisions are made and where key government institutions are located. National spatial planning regulations—specifically Government Regulation No. 26/2008 (revised by Government Regulation No. 13/2017)—designate Bandung as part of the National Activity Center (PKN) of the Greater Bandung Metropolitan Area, reinforcing its role as a hub for economic, administrative, and social activities. In addition, Presidential Regulation No. 45/2018 identifies Bandung as the core zone of the Bandung Basin National Strategic Area (KSN).

Economically, Bandung is one of West Java’s largest urban economies, supported by strong industrial sectors such as textiles, electronics, and fashion, as well as a vibrant commercial landscape. The city is also a major center for higher education and research, home to leading institutions like ITB, Unpad, and UPI, which attract students from across Indonesia and abroad and contribute to technological innovation and skilled workforce development. Culturally and historically rich, Bandung is a major tourist destination known for its natural attractions, shopping districts, and culinary scene, all of which significantly contribute to regional tourism. The city also serves as a national hub for technology, creativity, and digital innovation through spaces like Bandung Digital Valley and EduCity. Its strategic location in central Java makes Bandung an important transportation node, connected by major highways, rail networks, and Husein Sastranegara International Airport.

Finally, Bandung has an important environmental role, facing key sustainability challenges while implementing green initiatives to manage natural resources and maintain ecological balance. Overall, Bandung remains a vital center of governance, economy, education, culture, technology, and sustainability, with strong potential for continued development in the future.

City Development Focus:

Bandung’s tourism sector is a major part of its identity and economy. According to the official Invest Bandung site, arts, entertainment, and recreation are central to the city’s appeal:

  • in 2024, Bandung recorded 8.59 million visitor arrivals, supported by more than 3,000 food businesses and 440 hotels.
  • From a statistical perspective, the Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS) Bandung reports that in September 2025, there were 1,896,320 domestic tourist trips to the city, while hotel occupancy (for both star-rated and non-star-rated hotels) was about 50.90%.
  • At the provincial level, West Java’s Tourism and Culture Office publishes monthly tourism data, showing that Bandung is a key destination within the province.

  • 1. Smart City Initiative
    Bandung has implemented a comprehensive Smart City program focused on six dimensions: Smart Governance, Smart Society, Smart Branding, Smart Living, Smart Economy, and Smart Environment. According to the Bandung City Government, the city has a smart city roadmap with phased development; for example, their “Smart World Living Lab” and “Bandung Technopolis” are part of long-term plans.
  • 2. Digital Governance & Data Integration
    In December 2024, the Bandung City Government (Diskominfo) launched two major digital platforms: Sipedasi (an integrated data management system) and a PMO (Project Management Office) application for IT project governance.
  • 3. Long-Term Innovation Sustainability Strategy
    Academic research notes that Bandung’s local government fosters a culture of continuous innovation by building ecosystems that include academia, business, communities, and government (a “quad-helix” model).

Opportunities for Collaboration:

Bandung offers significant potential to strengthen social, economic, and cultural ties between the cities in the spirit of Asian-African solidarity.

  • Bandung City Government together with ITB hosted a Bandung Asia-Africa City Network Symposium, bringing together ambassadors, city leaders, academics, and business actors from Asian and African countries. The theme was “collaborative and innovative approaches to climate change, urbanization, and water crisis,” showing Bandung’s ambition to cooperate internationally on major urban and environmental challenges.

  • Bandung is recognized as a “creative city” in the UNESCO Creative Cities Network, which fosters cooperation among cities globally based on creative economy, culture, and sustainability. This gives Bandung a strong foundation for cultural and creative collaborations.

  • Tourism development in regions surrounding Bandung (for example, in Situ Cileunca) has been studied for its impacts on land use. This suggests opportunities for more sustainable planning and regional collaboration on environmental conservation.
