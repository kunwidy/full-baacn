Bandung Asia Africa City Network Symposium 2025

By: Atep Burhanudin

The city of Bandung has once again become the axis of global diplomacy through the 2025 Bandung Asia Africa City Network (BAACN) Symposium

which lasted for two days, May 19-20, 2025. With the theme "Realizing Collaboration in Facing the Challenges of Climate Change, Urbanization, and the Clean Water Crisis through Collaborative and Innovative Approaches," the event brought together various stakeholders from cities in Asia and Africa in the spirit of solidarity and innovation.

The Acting Regional Secretary of the City of Bandung, Iskandar Zulkarnain, in his official report said that this symposium is a strategic forum to encourage collaboration between cities from Asian and African countries, especially through strengthening city branding based on local culture and the development of cultural tourism.

"This forum facilitates the exchange of experiences and best practices between cities facing similar challenges in climate change, urbanization, and the clean water crisis. It is hoped that from this real collaboration will be born that connects cities in Asia and Africa in concrete projects," he said in the West Hall of the Bandung Institute of Technology (ITB), Bandung City, Monday (19/5/2025).

In addition to highlighting climate and clean water challenges, the event also introduced the Indonesia Water Warriors initiative as a collaborative platform between the government, youth, the private sector, academia, and non-governmental organizations for sustainable solutions to the water crisis. Participants who attended this symposium came from various circles, ranging from central and local government officials, representatives of embassies and cities from Asian and African countries, members of APEKSI, Forkopimda, business actors, to communities and academics.

In a series of events, participants participated in a number of thematic discussion sessions, including:- Promoting Center of Excellence and City Branding- Cultural Tourism and City Networking- Climate Change and Resilient CityNot only that, participants were also invited to explore the Footprint of Asia Africa in a city tour, and enjoy a Gala Dinner at the Bandung Mayor Hall as a place for cultural diplomacy and strengthening informal networks between cities.

The speakers in this activity came from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Ministry of Culture, the Ministry of Tourism, as well as academics from Tokyo City University, the Indonesian Institute of Cultural Arts (ISBI), and Pasundan University.The BAACN 2025 Symposium is expected to give birth to a joint commitment between cities in Asia and Africa to learn from each other, share resources, and build collaborative projects in the face of increasingly complex global challenges. "We hope that through this forum, a network of resilient and inclusive cities will be formed, and concrete cooperation will be born in the fields of environment, infrastructure, education, and cultural tourism," he said.
