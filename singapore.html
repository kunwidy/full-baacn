Singapore

5.638.700 Resident

Singapore

1.28967° N - 103.85° E

Population Characteristics:

The social characteristics of Singapore's population include high multiculturalism, with ethnic Chinese as the majority, followed by Malays and Indians.

Their society places a strong emphasis on social hierarchy and respect for elders and those in authority, while upholding the principles of group cohesion and meritocracy. However, they are also known for their entrepreneurial spirit and pragmatism. There is a strong respect for hierarchy, especially in family relationships, education, and work, where age and social status are highly valued. Singaporean society places great emphasis on group values, which are reflected in interdependence and a sense of shared responsibility.

City's Unique Identity:

Singapore's cultural icons are potent symbols that embody the nation's heritage, identity, and values. The most prominent among these is the Merlion , a mythical creature with a lion's head and a fish's body, serving as the official mascot and national personification of Singapore. The fish body represents the city's humble beginnings as a fishing village, while the lion head pays homage to its ancient name "Singapura," meaning "Lion City" in Sanskrit. Another significant cultural icon is the Vanda Miss Joaquim orchid hybrid , which is the national flower and a symbol of the nation's uniqueness and resilience. The national symbols, including the National Flag with its crescent moon and five stars, represent core principles such as democracy, peace, and equality, reinforcing a shared sense of national identity among its multi-ethnic population.

Singapore's landmarks are a striking blend of historical structures and futuristic architectural marvels that define its modern skyline. One of the most famous modern landmarks is the Marina Bay Sands resort complex, recognizable by its unique design featuring three hotel towers topped by a massive ship-shaped SkyPark. Nearby, the Gardens by the Bay stands as an icon of biophilic urban design, known for its colossal, solar-powered Supertree Grove and climate-controlled conservatories like the Cloud Forest and Flower Dome . Historic landmarks that offer a glimpse into the nation's past include the Raffles Hotel , a national monument famous for introducing the Singapore Sling cocktail, and the Sultan Mosque in Kampong Glam, a testament to the city-state's Muslim heritage and architectural diversity. The Merlion Park , while home to a cultural icon, also functions as a major landmark, offering a popular vantage point for the stunning Marina Bay cityscape.

  • Chinese New Year (January/February): A huge celebration in Chinatown with lanterns, dragon dances, and traditional food.
  • Chingay Parade : A street parade festival featuring magnificent costumes, dancing, and floats.
  • i Light Marina Bay : An arts festival featuring eco-friendly light installations around Marina Bay.
  • Singapore Night Festival : A night festival in the Bras Basah-Bugis district featuring art performances, light installations and music.
  • Singapore International Festival of Arts (SIFA) : A performing arts festival showcasing various arts disciplines from within and outside the country.

Singapore has a variety of culinary delights that are a must try, such asHainanese Chicken Rice , Chili Crab , and Laksa which has become a culinary icon of the country. Other options include Kaya Toast , Roti Prata , Satay , Char Kway Teow , and Bak Kut Teh.

The main local language in Singapore is Singlish, a creole English that is a mixture of English, Malay, Mandarin, and Tamil. Besides Singlish, the official languages ​​of Singapore are English, Mandarin, Malay, and Tamil, with Malay being the national language.

The Role of Cities in a National or Regional Context:

  • Trade and Logistics Hub: Leveraging its strategic geographical location on major shipping lanes, Singapore developed the world's busiest container port and became a global transportation hub (sea and air).
  • Financial and Services Hub: Singapore is a leading business and financial services hub in Southeast Asia, attracting foreign direct investment (FDI) which is a major driver of its economic growth.
  • High Value Added Industries: Focus on the manufacturing sector, especially electronics and petrochemicals, as well as the service and tourism industries.
  • Innovation and Quality Human Resources: Large investments in education and training, as well as an efficient and transparent public bureaucracy, ensure a competent workforce and an environment conducive to innovation.

City Development Focus:

Singapore tourism development driven by a strong post-pandemic recovery, marked by increased tourist numbers and revenues in 2023 and 2024Singapore continues to innovate, launching new attractions like Bird Paradise and Sensoryscape on Sentosa Island , and strengthening its position as a sustainable tourism destination by being certified as the first country to implement the Global Sustainable Tourism Council (GSTC) sustainability criteria. The hospitality industry is also growing, with new hotel openings and major renovations.

  • NEWater : This is wastewater that has been reprocessed into very high-quality drinking water through advanced filtration processes, including microfiltration, reverse osmosis, and ultraviolet disinfection. NEWater now supplies the majority of Singapore's non-potable water needs (primarily for industry) and is a cornerstone of the country's water security.
  • Desalination : Singapore is also investing heavily in desalination technology (processing seawater into fresh water) to diversify its water sources.
  • Rainwater Harvesting : Efficient rainwater harvesting systems are spread throughout the island.
  • Vehicle Title Certificate (COE) : The government limits the number of new cars registered by requiring prospective car owners to purchase expensive COEs through auction, making car ownership very expensive.
  • Electronic Road Tolling (ERP) : Singapore uses an electronic toll collection system to charge motorists entering congested downtown areas during peak hours, helping to manage traffic in real-time.
  • Extensive Public Transport Infrastructure : Heavy investment in an efficient and integrated Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) and bus network makes it the preferred choice for most residents.

  • Nationwide sustainability agenda & targets. Singapore’s Green Plan 2030 is a whole-of-government roadmap with concrete targets (e.g., quadrupling solar deployment, planting one million trees, reducing landfill by 30%) that guide public and private action on climate, energy, and green growth.
  • Advanced urban greenery and biodiversity programs. The National Parks Board (NParks) reports that over 40% of Singapore is covered by greenery (nature reserves, parks, skyrise greenery), improving urban liveability, biodiversity, and public well-being.
  • World-class water management & circularity. PUB (Singapore’s national water agency) publishes detailed sustainability reports showing resilient water supply systems, wastewater reuse (NEWater), and programs to decarbonise water services, benefiting public health and resource security.

Opportunities for Collaboration:

Cooperation between Singapore and Bandung holds significant promise to deepen social, economic, and cultural ties.

Share Smart Nation tools and governance models (digital IDs, e-services, data platforms) to improve Bandung’s municipal service delivery and participatory planning.

Technical exchange on water reuse, flood resilience, and integrated waste management (PUB’s NEWater and circularity practice are strong models for tropical, high-density cities). Bandung could pilot reuse or decentralized treatment solutions with Singaporean technical partners.

Transfer NParks’ skyrise-greenery, park design, and tree-planting methodologies to accelerate Bandung’s green corridors, public-space upgrades, and urban cooling programs.

Singapore is a regional green-finance hub (public/private investors, climate funds). Bandung and other KAA cities could co-develop bankable green projects (energy, transport, resilience) and access blended finance or carbon-investment platforms.
